Literature Survey

Coding has become an essential 21st-century skill, promoting problem-solving, creativity, and computational thinking across disciplines. However, traditional teaching methods often focus on syntax and correctness rather than creativity, exploration, or persistence, creating a gap between classroom learning and real-world coding skills.

Adaptive learning systems have emerged to address these issues by personalizing instruction through AI and data analytics. Platforms like Knewton and Duolingo demonstrate how learning content can adjust dynamically to each student’s needs, though most still ignore emotional factors that affect engagement.

Recent advances in AI and machine learning now enable deeper personalization, including emotion-aware systems that detect frustration or disengagement and adapt lessons accordingly. In parallel, teacher-support dashboards provide real-time analytics to guide instruction.

Despite progress, existing systems often operate in isolation—focusing on either cognitive, emotional, or analytic adaptivity. There remains a need for a unified, school-focused platform that integrates adaptive assessment, emotional intelligence, and teacher insights to create more inclusive and effective coding education experiences.

Research Gap

Although coding is increasingly taught in schools, current teaching and assessment methods fail to reflect how students truly learn programming. Lessons often focus on whether code “works” rather than on creativity, reasoning, or persistence—penalizing curious learners who explore and rewarding rote memorization.

Adaptive learning systems have begun to personalize education, yet in coding they remain limited and fragmented. Most platforms track only cognitive performance, overlooking emotional factors like frustration or motivation. Advanced AI tools capable of generating personalized exercises and feedback are underused, while affective computing technologies that can detect and respond to learner emotions are rarely integrated.

Teachers, too, receive minimal actionable insights from learner data. Current dashboards summarize completion rates but fail to highlight misconceptions or guide instruction.

What’s missing is a unified, AI-powered ecosystem that combines adaptive assessment, emotion recognition, generative AI, multimodal scaffolding, and teacher analytics. This study aims to fill that gap by creating a holistic learning platform that supports both cognitive and emotional growth, making coding education more inclusive, equitable, and effective.

Research Problem

Coding is now a fundamental skill, yet traditional teaching and assessment methods are outdated. Current approaches focus on whether code runs correctly, neglecting creativity, problem-solving strategies, and perseverance. This rigid system misrepresents students’ abilities, leaving fast learners bored and struggling students overwhelmed.

While adaptive learning platforms and AI tools exist, they remain fragmented—tracking only accuracy or speed, generating exercises in isolation, or providing raw data without actionable insights. Emotional factors such as frustration or disengagement, which strongly affect learning, are rarely addressed. Teachers, too, lack meaningful guidance to support diverse learners.

The core problem is the absence of a unified, AI-powered adaptive system that:

Personalizes learning with intelligent question generation and dynamic difficulty.

Recognizes and responds to students’ emotional states.

Supports multiple learning styles and abilities.

Provides actionable insights for teachers.

Operates securely in classroom settings.

Without such integration, coding education fails to meet students’ cognitive, emotional, and individual needs, limiting engagement, equity, and meaningful learning outcomes.

Research Objectives

Main Objective

The main goal of this study is to design, develop, and evaluate an AI-powered adaptive learning system for school students aged 10–12. Unlike traditional “one-size-fits-all” coding instruction, this platform will personalize learning in real time by adjusting difficulty, content, and support based on each student’s performance and engagement.

The system will integrate multiple AI techniques, including adaptive assessment generation, skill modeling, affective computing, biometric authentication, and teacher-facing analytics, creating a holistic ecosystem. This approach addresses current gaps in coding education by:

Delivering personalized, responsive learning experiences.

Detecting and responding to students’ emotional states.

Providing actionable insights to educators to guide instruction.

Methodology

This research develops an interactive, gamified web-based Learning Management System (LMS) to teach Python programming to Grade 6 students in Sri Lanka. The system integrates gamification, adaptive learning, and AI-driven educational technology to provide curriculum-aligned, engaging, and personalized content.

Key Features:

Gamified Lessons: Programming concepts (variables, loops, conditionals) are delivered as challenges, quizzes, and puzzles with points, badges, and rewards to encourage persistence and problem-solving.


Learning Materials: Videos and downloadable resources aligned with the Grade 6 ICT syllabus allow self-paced study, while teachers can add supplementary content.


Embedded Python Compiler: Students can code and execute programs directly on the platform, receiving instant feedback to reinforce learning.


Adaptive Feedback & Analytics: Student performance is continuously monitored, offering personalized suggestions, while teachers access dashboards to track progress and adjust instruction.


Teacher Support & Curriculum Alignment: Reports and dashboards reduce workload and align with the National Education Framework (2021).


Scalability & Security: Supports 1,000+ simultaneous users, with role-based authentication and optional face recognition.


Development Process: Built using an Agile Scrum approach with the MERN stack for frontend, Flask for AI model integration, and MongoDB for storage. AI models (GPT-2 for question generation, CNN for emotion recognition) are trained, validated, and integrated iteratively to ensure robustness and responsiveness.
